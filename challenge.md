# Cybersecurity & Risk Analysis: Securing Critical Infrastructure Supporting Data Centers

**Co-Sponsored by Mr. James Elliott & Microsoft**

## Scenario

Northern Virginia hosts the world’s largest concentration of data centers, powering rapidly growing AI and cloud services. These facilities demand immense electricity and water for continuous operation and cooling. Local utilities rely on OT systems (SCADA, PLCs, industrial sensors) to manage infrastructure, pumps, valves, and substations serving commercial and residential customers.

Many OT systems are decades old and not designed with cybersecurity, making them attractive targets for threat actors. Recent campaigns including Volt Typhoon, Sandworm, and CyberAv3ngers have targeted U.S. critical infrastructure (water, energy, transportation, manufacturing). Disruption risks data centers, governments, and the economy, and may cause physical damage and safety hazards.

Royal Duke Infrastructure Group (RDIG) provides electricity and industrial water to data centers in Loudoun, Prince William, and Fairfax counties, Virginia. Per federal OT asset security guidance, RDIG requests a Cyber Risk Assessment and Mitigation Plan to identify vulnerabilities, prioritize risk, and recommend cost-effective safeguards.

---

## Challenge

Using credible government, academic, and industry sources, evaluate major cybersecurity risks for RDIG’s operational systems and propose practical, business-oriented risk reduction strategies.

### Deliverables

- 5–10 minute video presentation (`.mp4`) summarizing findings and recommendations
- 1-page professional report (`.pdf`) documenting key points and visuals supporting analysis

Deliverables should show clear understanding of RDIG context, OT risk, and feasible recommendations.

### Professional Report Requirements

1. **Current Situation Summary**
   - RDIG operational role and dependencies
   - Why OT systems are vulnerable and relevant cyber threats
   - Regional context (e.g., rising electricity demand for data centers) and disruption consequences

2. **Risk Identification**
   - At least three realistic OT cybersecurity risks
   - For each: occurrence scenario, potential impact (financial, operational, safety, reputation), likelihood (low/medium/high)

3. **Impact and Cost–Benefit Analysis**
   - Estimate potential business/operational impact from cyber incidents
   - At least three safeguards/mitigations with description, relative cost, estimated benefit
   - How RDIG balances investment, reliability, resilience

4. **Recommendations & Roadmap**
   - Prioritized recommendations
   - Short-, mid-, long-term timeline

5. **References**
   - Credible, properly formatted sources

### Presentation Requirements

1. **Format**
   - PowerPoint, Google Slides, Canva, Prezi, or similar
   - Clear visuals, consistent style, citations

2. **Content**
   - Overview: context and problem statement
   - Key Risks: top 3 risks (icons/risk matrix)
   - Threat Scenarios: attack paths + consequences
   - Recommendations: 3 safeguards with cost/benefit
   - Roadmap: short/mid/long-term actions
   - Business Impact Summary: risk posture and resilience improvements

3. **References**
   - Credible sources
